Techno-Religion, Quantum Biology, and AI-Powered Research

This week's collection explores the rise of a new techno-religion in Silicon Valley, revolutionary discoveries in quantum biology, and significant leaps in AI-powered drug research. From spiritual evolution to quantum computing in the brain, we're witnessing rapid advancements that challenge our understanding of life, intelligence, and the future of technology.

  1. The Rise of Silicon Valley's Techno-Religion
    The Rationalists, a community focused on the risks of artificial intelligence, regularly gather with tech figures and other like-minded people in a complex that covers much of a city block. Explore at The New York Times
  2. The Bright Side of Intel Exodus: Chip Startups

    SpiNNcloud has developed a brain-inspired supercomputer with 650,000 cores, revolutionizing drug discovery and personalized medicine. Discover at Rude Baguette
  4. Quantum Computing Occurs Naturally in the Human Brain, Study Finds
    Tryptophan networks in cells may act as quantum computers, reshaping biology, neuroscience, and the search for life in the universe. Read more at The Brighter Side of News
  5. Quantum Scheme Protects Videos From Prying Eyes and Tampering
    Researchers have used quantum physics to reversibly scramble videos so that even future quantum computers won't be able to hack them. Explore at The Conversation
  6. Anthropic Revokes OpenAI's Access to Claude
    OpenAI lost access to the Claude API this week after Anthropic claimed the company was violating its terms of service. Learn more at WIRED
  7. The "Electron Shower" That's Revolutionizing Microchip Manufacturing
    Scientists have cracked a long-standing challenge in thin-film electronics, potentially transforming microchip production. Discover at SciTechDaily
  8. Scientists Use Quantum Machine Learning to Create Semiconductors for the First Time
    Researchers have found a way to make the chip design and manufacturing process much easier by tapping into a hybrid blend of artificial intelligence and quantum computing. Read more at Live Science
  9. Canada's Tech Heavyweights Are Planting Flags in the UK
    Shared values, deep talent, and strong government support are drawing Canadian firms like Cohere and OpenText to the United Kingdom. Explore at BetaKit
  10. Fresh Off A $37.5M Round, Augmodo's Vision for Spatial Computing Inside Retail Stores Sharpens
    Augmodo's spatial AI tech, worn by workers in stores, tracks inventory data on retail store shelves. Learn more at GeekWire
