🌈💫 The Less You Know

The More You Learn

Latest Technologies

  • Bicycle forces and mechanics: The study of the forces and mechanical aspects that affect bicycle performance, ride quality, and safety.
  • Passthrough video usage in mixed reality: The use of live video feed from the real world in mixed reality experiences, allowing users to see and interact with their physical surroundings while immersed in a virtual environment.
  • Metaverse: A virtual reality space where users can interact with a computer-generated environment and other users in real time, often described as a collective virtual shared space.
  • Artificial Intelligence (AI): The development of computer systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making.
  • AI-generated art: Artistic creations generated or influenced by artificial intelligence algorithms, where machines play a role in the creative process.
  • Mixed Reality: The merging of real and virtual worlds to produce new environments and visualizations where physical and digital objects coexist and interact in real time.
  • Quantum Computing: A field of computing that utilizes quantum bits (qubits) to perform complex calculations exponentially faster than classical computers, potentially revolutionizing cryptography, drug discovery, and optimization problems.
  • Spatial Computing: The integration of digital information and virtual objects into the physical world, allowing users to interact with and manipulate digital content in real-world environments.

Most Important Topics

  • Energy Optimization: The process of maximizing energy efficiency and minimizing waste through intelligent systems and strategies. 
  • Sustainability: The practice of using resources in a way that meets present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
  • Renewable Energy: Energy derived from sources that are naturally replenished, such as solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal power. 
  • Smarter Consumption: The concept of using technology, data, and behavioral changes to optimize resource usage and minimize environmental impact.

Acronym

  • XR: Extended Reality, an umbrella term encompassing virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and mixed reality (MR).
  • AI: Artificial Intelligence
  • HVAC: Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning

Technical Terms

  • Predictive Maintenance: A proactive maintenance strategy that uses data analysis and machine learning to predict when equipment or systems are likely to fail, allowing for timely maintenance and minimizing downtime.
  • Carbon Footprint: The total amount of greenhouse gases, primarily carbon dioxide, emitted directly or indirectly by an individual, organization, product, or event throughout its lifecycle.
  • Cognitive Load: The mental effort required to process information and perform tasks, which can impact cognitive performance and decision-making.
  • Quantum mechanics: A branch of physics that describes the behavior of matter and energy at the smallest scales, often defying classical physics principles.
  • G-Force: A measurement of the force exerted on an object by the acceleration due to gravity, often experienced during high-speed or high-acceleration movements.
  • Compositing: The process of combining visual elements from separate sources, such as CG characters and live-action footage, to create a final image or scene.

Each of these topics represents a facet of the rapidly evolving technological landscape, offering both opportunities for advancement and challenges that need careful consideration.
