"Get Real! You're 20 Years Late and the Problem is 200 Times Worse"

While government fumbles with 20-year-old ID programs, tech billionaires deploy iris scanners in malls across America. This isn't coincidence—it's a calculated power grab for the ultimate prize: control of your digital existence itself.

As federal agencies finally implement REAL ID requirements that were conceived in 2005, private corporations have already raced far ahead, building sophisticated biometric identity systems that operate with minimal oversight. Worldcoin's iris scanning kiosks have appeared in shopping centers nationwide, while facial recognition networks quietly expand through retail spaces, airports, and public venues. The contrast couldn't be more stark: government struggles with basic identity verification while tech firms rapidly construct privatized identity infrastructures that commoditize your most personal biological data.
