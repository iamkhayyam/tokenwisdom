Evolution, Infrastructure, and Intelligence: When Biology Meets Computing

This week's collection reveals how ancient biological patterns inform cutting-edge technology while new hardware architectures challenge established players in the race to build tomorrow's intelligence.

  1. Sea Anemones Share Our Body-Building Techniques
    Despite having no brain, sea anemones use developmental techniques previously thought unique to bilaterians like humans, suggesting fundamental biological patterns are older than we imagined. Read more at Popular Mechanics
  2. Mythic Rebounds with $125M for Analog AI Chips
    Once-struggling startup Mythic secures massive funding to develop analog computing for AI, promising better token-processing efficiency than Nvidia's dominant GPUs. Read more at EE Times
  3. Standalone Spin-Wave Chip Breakthrough
    Researchers create the first integrated spin-wave device operating without external magnets, potentially revolutionizing telecommunications beyond current 5G and 6G standards. Read more at Phys.org
  4. AI Chip Startup Etched Raises $500M
    Peter Thiel backs Etched in a massive funding round valuing the Nvidia challenger at $5 billion, as the race for AI infrastructure intensifies. Read more at Yahoo Finance
  5. Meta Taps Wall Street Executive as President
    Zuckerberg's choice of Dina Powell McCormick as Meta's president wins praise from Trump, signaling potential political alignment as tech platforms navigate regulatory pressures. Read more at Washington Post
  6. Supercomputers Approach Human Brain Simulation
    The world's most powerful computing systems can now model billions of neurons, bringing researchers closer to unprecedented insights into brain function. Read more at New Scientist
  7. Hard Fork Host Predicts AI Math Breakthrough
    NYT podcast host Kevin Roose makes a high-confidence prediction that an AI company will solve one of mathematics' most challenging Millennium Prize Problems in 2026. Read more at New York Times
  8. Origami Mathematics Connects to Particle Physics
    The amplituhedron, a shape central to particle physics calculations, shows surprising connections to paper-folding mathematics, potentially simplifying complex quantum computations. Read more at Quanta Magazine
  9. AI's Memorization Crisis Exposed
    Research reveals large language models don't truly "learn" but instead memorize training data, raising fundamental questions about AI development strategies. Read more at The Atlantic
  10. Iran Deploys Starlink "Kill Switch"
    During widespread protests, Iranian authorities demonstrate previously unknown capability to disable Starlink internet services, worsening communication blackouts. Read more at Forbes

Building Without Blueprints

🎯 HOOK: AI didn't break democracy. We broke democracy. Not with algorithms or deep fakes, but with spreadsheets and quarterly earnings reports that turned public goods into private profit centers.

🎣 LINE: While we worried about robots taking our jobs, corporations were already dismantling education, healthcare, and journalism—methodically converting civic infrastructure into extraction opportunities. The machine didn't go rogue; the machine is working exactly as designed.

🔱 SINKER: ChatGPT didn't create a crisis—it merely inherited one. The question isn't whether AI will destroy our institutions; it's whether anything will remain worth saving when the last public good has been privatized and the last civic voice has been monetized.

"AI isn't the villain—it's the mirror. It reflects back the consequences of decades of choices we made to defund, privatize, and commodify public goods."
