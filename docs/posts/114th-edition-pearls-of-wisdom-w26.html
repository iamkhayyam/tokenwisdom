  1. Companies That Replaced Humans With AI Are Realizing Their Mistake
    As AI agents have yet to pay for themselves, more and more executives are waking up to the sloppy reality of AI hype. Read more at Futurism
  2. 'World's Oldest Pyramid' Built 25,000 Years Ago Was Not Made by Humans
    While Guinness World Records officially lists the Djoser Step pyramid in Egypt as the world's oldest pyramid (around 2,630 BC), one paper published in October claimed a layer of the Gunung Padang pyramid in Indonesia was constructed as far back as 25,000 BC. Learn more at Indy100
  3. "Like Nothing Ever Seen Before": Astronomers Discover Colossal Milky Way Cloud
    Astronomers discovered a colossal molecular cloud named M4.7-0.8 in the Milky Way, weighing as much as 160,000 suns and located 23,000 light-years away. Discover the details at Sustainability Times
  4. "Remarkable" Pattern Discovered Behind Prime Numbers
    It "represents theoretical math that could've been done decades ago" - but it has big implications for the future of mathematics and cryptography. Understand the impact at IFLScience
  5. They Did It: Teleportation Becomes a Scientific Reality
    A photon, teleported through the Internet, could well disrupt our conception of the digital world and communications forever. Read the breakthrough at 3DVF
  6. SpinLaunch Catapults Satellites Into Orbit Without Using Fuel
    A giant slingshot might sound like science fiction — but one California startup is turning this idea into reality with revolutionary satellite deployment. Explore now at The Brighter Side
  7. Meta in Talks to Partially Acquire VC Fund NFDG
    Meta explores acquiring part of NFDG venture capital fund, which operates its own supercomputer, the Andromeda Cluster. Unpack the insights at Data Center Dynamics
  8. From Niche to Billion Pound Industry: How Immersive Events Have Taken Over
    Despite financially tough times post-pandemic, the continued popularity of experience events could indicate consumers are being choosier about their spending. Explore the implications at Sky News
  9. Midlife Caffeine Intake Linked to Healthy Aging
    A long-term study following nearly 50,000 women over 30 years found that drinking caffeinated coffee in midlife is linked to healthier aging. Read the findings at Neuroscience News
  10. An Open Letter to Cannes Lions: You Need to Do More for Brand Experience
    Experiential marketing is driving growth for the whole industry, yet it's not being properly recognized at the industry's biggest awards show. Understand the approach at The Drum
