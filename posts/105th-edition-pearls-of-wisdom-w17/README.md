The Moral Bankruptcy of "Cheat on Everything" AI

W17 - In a world where 'Cheat on Everything' AI turns deceit into an art form, the real casualty is authenticity. Silicon Valley's latest darling teaches us not just how to fake it, but how to surrender the trust that glues our society together. Will we bank on deception or integrity?

The recent emergence of products like Cluely, marketed explicitly as "invisible AI to cheat on everything," reveals a troubling vision of technology's future. As AI-enabled deception becomes increasingly indistinguishable from authentic interaction, we face profound questions about trust, integrity, and the value we place on genuine human connection. The promotional video for Cluely parallels similar developments across industries—from simulated knowledge to manufactured intimacy—where the line between genuine and artificial blurs to near invisibility.

The fundamental tension emerges not from the technology itself but from the intentional deception involved. Society doesn't necessarily object to technological assistance, but it does expect transparency. When companies explicitly market their products as tools for cheating, they violate an implicit social contract and devalue the very trust infrastructure that makes meaningful connection possible.
