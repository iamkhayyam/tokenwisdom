Technical Terms:

 * Layer-wise scaling strategy: A method used in neural network training aimed at managing computational complexity and improving model efficiency by adjusting the size of the model layers.
 * Consumer Deceleration: This term denotes a shift in consumer behavior towards slower consumption methods, prioritizing quality and awareness over speed and convenience.
 * Digital Precision: In the context of technology, refers to the high degree of accuracy and detail that digital processes can achieve compared to traditional methods.
 * Happy Accidents: In art and technology, refers to unexpected but beneficial outcomes that occur without intent, often valued for their uniqueness and creativity.
 * Cultural Justice: Advocating for fairness and rectification in the ways cultural representations and artifacts are handled, often referring to returning items taken during colonial times.
 * Bio-Inspired LEDs: LEDs designed based on biological models, such as fireflies, to improve efficiency in light emission.
 * Multimaterial 3D Printing: A form of 3D printing that utilizes multiple types of materials in a single print process to create composite structures or devices.
 * Light Extraction Efficiency: A measure of how effectively a lighting device converts electricity into visible light.
 * Eddy Current Losses: Losses in electrical machines due to the formation of electric currents induced by changing magnetic fields in conductive materials.
 * Digital Twin Technology: Utilizing virtual models to simulate, predict, and optimize the functions of physical entities in the digital world.
