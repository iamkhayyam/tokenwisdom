 * Ideal Glass: A theoretical amorphous solid at thermodynamic equilibrium with minimum configurational entropy — the most ordered disordered material permitted by the Second Law; demonstrated experimentally for the first time this week under conditions that cannot be maintained at scale
 * Shannon-Wakil Effect: A structural phenomenon identified in a February 2026 mathematics paper in which an exponential configuration space undergoes forced dimensional reduction to a strict subspace governed by a constant uniquely determined by the algebraic structure of the system; proven to occur in Shannon's 1948 information theory results and in a new result in prime arithmetic
 * 4D Imaging Sensor: A chip combining LiDAR depth sensing and motion capture on a single device, producing continuous spatial models of moving environments without post-hoc data fusion
 * Sparse Attention (DeepSeek): An architectural innovation that reduces long-context AI processing cost by selectively attending to relevant tokens rather than computing full attention across all positions; approximately 80% cheaper than dense attention at equivalent context lengths
 * KadNap Botnet: A distributed cyber weapon assembled from hijacked devices across global networks; the first documented instance of this attack architecture, with victims spread worldwide
 * Cortical Labs / Biological Intelligence: DARPA-funded research growing living human neural tissue on silicon substrates to create hybrid biological-computational systems; demonstrated running interactive software

Acronyms:

 * LiDAR — Light Detection and Ranging: a sensing technology using pulsed laser light to measure distances and map environments in three dimensions
 * DARPA — Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency: the US Department of Defense agency responsible for developing emerging technologies for military use
 * KV Cache — Key-Value Cache: a memory structure in transformer AI models storing intermediate computations to avoid redundant processing during inference


Technical Terms:

 * Constitutional Forcing (Shannon-Wakil): The mechanism by which a system's intrinsic algebraic structure forces its configuration space to concentrate onto a strict subspace — the governing constant is not chosen but uniquely determined by the structure itself
 * Configurational Entropy: The component of entropy arising from the number of possible arrangements of atoms or molecules in a material; minimized in ideal glass by approaching thermodynamic equilibrium
 * Sparse Attention: An attention mechanism that computes relationships between a subset of token positions rather than all pairs; reduces quadratic scaling cost of full attention while preserving most information for relevant contexts
 * The Hard Problem of Consciousness: David Chalmers' formulation of the explanatory gap between physical descriptions of neural processes and the subjective experience of those processes; not solved by any current theory
 * Dominance Reasoning vs. Expected Value (Newcomb, revisited): The unresolved tension in decision theory between choosing what's best in all scenarios (dominance) and choosing what has the highest probability-weighted outcome (expected value) — relevant to any AI system making decisions under uncertainty
